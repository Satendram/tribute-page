Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Official portrait, 2002, 11th President of India, Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India in 2002.

Story of Missile man

  • Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam

  • Young age: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 to a Tamil Muslim family in the pilgrimage centre of Rameswaram on Pamban Island, then in the Madras Presidency and now in the State of Tamil Nadu.

  • Career as a scientist: After graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960, Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation as a scientist.

  • From 25 July 2002 to 25 July 2007: Kalam served as the 11th President of India, succeeding K. R. Narayanan.

  • Post-presidency: After leaving office, Kalam became a visiting professor at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.

  • Death: On 27 July 2015, His last words, to his aide Srijan Pal Singh, were reportedly: "Funny guy! Are you doing well?

  • Memorial: The Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam National Memorial was built in memory of Kalam by the DRDO in Pei Karumbu.
